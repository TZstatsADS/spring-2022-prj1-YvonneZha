Yvonne Zha lz2806

Can Schools in Philosophy represent the similarity between philosophers?

Speaking of great philosophers, you may come up with Plato, Aristotle, etc. However, how will you explain the relationship of these philosophers? Schools can be a good perspective to investigate into this problem. As a non-expert in philosophy, the very first question I would like to propose is: what does that School stands for here? Does School explain the similarity between philosophers, or its just a meaningless property? My hypothesis here would be: School can actually explain the similarity between philosophers. To verify my hypothesis, I decide to use Text Mining techiques, especially Topic Modeling, to cluter the philosophers, and finally compare the results with the schools which these philosophers belong to. Furthermore, I would also like to investigate the relationship between schools.

Does the clustering results coincides with schools? Take feminism as an example, Wollstonecraft, Beauvoir and Davis are perfectly clustered into one group. Clustering really does a good job here, according the fact that philosophers from the same school falls into the same cluster. Besides, the connection between schools can also be inferred from the cluster results. Empiricism and Rationalism are close, while feminism are different from other schools. We can further conduct cluster on schools to see if this still holds.
